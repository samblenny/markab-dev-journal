MacForth in Evolution of Forth

While reading a pdf scan of “The Evolution of Forth”, on page 18 (marked as p642 in the original), I found a reference to MacForth as “the first resident development system for the 128K Apple Macintosh, immediately after the Mac’s debut in January 1984”. Apparently MacForth provided full access to all the graphics routines in the Mac’s built-in Toolbox ROM.

The article further explains that lots of early Mac software was written in MacForth. So, I wondered, could I find any source code or documentation?

FIG GEnie archive with Forth source

I found this web mirror of an ftp mirror of a FIG filesharing area on the GEnie dialup service from way back in the day:

https://www.complang.tuwien.ac.at/forth/ftp.dei.isep.ipp.pt/pub/forth/

There is a subdirectory specifically for Mac, which has a number of .ARC and .sit.hqx archive files containing Forth source code:

https://www.complang.tuwien.ac.at/forth/ftp.dei.isep.ipp.pt/pub/forth/mac/

Unpacking old Macintosh archives

This is what I came up with for unpacking the archive files on Ubuntu:

sudo apt install nomarch macutils unar
man nomarch
man hexbin
man unar
man lsar
nomarch whatever.arc
hexbin -3 whatever.hqx
unar whatever.sit.hqx
